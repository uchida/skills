# Asking Questions

A question exists to build common ground; the curse of knowledge works against it — your question and its option labels are pointers into analysis only you have seen. Write for a reader who did not watch you work.

1. **Ground the question** — what is being decided and what hangs on the answer; what raised it, and what you ruled out and why.
2. **Expand the options** — for each, what it is and what you will do if it is chosen; across them, what picking one gives up that another keeps.

Done when neither question nor labels need your context to decode, and the deciding differences are on the page.
